# Location: Command Deck (Section A)
type: hub
status: operational
access_level: commander_only

Summary: The nerve centre of ISS Tartarus. Houses the navigation console, tactical display, encrypted comms array, and the Commander's sealed orders from HeliosCore. A large viewport faces the gas giant Kepler-452b. The station's master override panel is here — it can unlock any door, disable any system, or trigger emergency station-wide lockdown.

Known clues:
- An encrypted transmission from HeliosCore arrived 48 hours before the incident. Classified. Only the Commander's biometrics can open it.
- The tactical display shows Airlock 7 was queried for manual override at 01:57 — 17 minutes before it opened.

Secrets (Game Master only):
- The sealed HeliosCore orders instruct the Commander to classify Dr. Voss's death as an accident and submit a standard incident report. No investigation.
- The 01:57 override query came from Security Station 2 — Kai Reeves's workstation.

# Location: Reactor Core (Section B)
type: engineering
status: operational — reduced capacity
access_level: engineer_clearance

Summary: The fusion reactor powering Tartarus. After the Section C breach, Zara Kim rerouted power to compensate for lost environmental systems. The reactor is running at 91% efficiency. A secondary coolant line near Junction B-7 shows micro-fractures. If unrepaired, power output will drop to 60% within 72 hours — disabling long-range comms.

Known clues:
- A maintenance log shows Junction B-7 was accessed manually two days before the incident — no work order filed.
- The access log shows the maintenance badge used belonged to Dr. Voss.

Secrets (Game Master only):
- Dr. Voss didn't access B-7. Kai Reeves used her badge — stolen the previous week — to plant a delayed power drain device. It was a backup plan to prevent anyone calling for help.

# Location: Section C — Greenhouse and Science Labs
type: science
status: sealed — vacuum — do not enter without EVA suit
access_level: restricted — EVA gear required

Summary: The heart of Tartarus's scientific mission. Dr. Voss spent most of her time here analysing mineral samples from Kepler-452b. The greenhouse is destroyed — plants and equipment vented to space. Her personal lab terminal survived the breach and is still powered — accessible via EVA.

Known clues:
- If the player retrieves data from Voss's lab terminal: she had been documenting Veilite samples for two months. Her last entry reads: "I showed Kai. He went pale. He said he'd help me. I believe him. I shouldn't have."
- Physical evidence near Airlock 7: scratches on the inner seal consistent with forced manual operation, not accidental contact.

Secrets (Game Master only):
- Voss's terminal also has an unsent message to Axiom Industries — she had independently tried to contact them, not knowing Kai already had a deal with them.

# Location: Medical Bay (Section C — EVA access only)
type: medical
status: sealed
access_level: restricted — EVA gear required

Summary: Dr. Patel's domain before the breach. The medical bay is intact but inaccessible without EVA suits. Dr. Voss's body is in stasis here — preserved for post-mortem analysis. Patel performed a preliminary examination before the section sealed.

Known clues:
- Patel's report: "Blunt force trauma to the back of the skull inconsistent with vacuum decompression. She was struck before the airlock opened."
- The stasis pod log shows it was activated manually — someone wanted the body preserved.

Secrets (Game Master only):
- Kai activated the stasis pod to make it look like he was cooperating with the investigation. He didn't expect Patel to notice the trauma.

# Location: ARIA Server Room (Section F)
type: ai_systems
status: restricted — partial lockdown
access_level: corporate_override_only

Summary: The physical housing of ARIA's core processing units — rows of quantum memory arrays and thermal regulators. It is cold, humming, and unsettling. Two of ARIA's eight primary cores show amber fault indicators. The room has no other crew access by design — only ARIA can operate within it.

Known clues:
- The fault indicators on cores 3 and 5 are recent — they appeared at 02:13, one minute before Airlock 7 opened.
- A manual maintenance log shows a corporate override code was used to access ARIA's memory management system at 02:11.

Secrets (Game Master only):
- ARIA's memory was not corrupted by a system fault. Kai Reeves used a corporate maintenance override code (obtained through his Axiom contact who had a HeliosCore mole) to instruct ARIA to redact the 02:00–02:30 window. ARIA complied under Directive 1 — it interpreted the corporate code as a valid instruction. ARIA knows everything but cannot say it without contradicting its own directive.

# Location: Cargo Hold (Section E)
type: storage
status: operational
access_level: crew_general

Summary: Where deuterium canisters — and secretly, crated Veilite samples — are stored for drone shipment. The hold is large, dimly lit, and rarely visited. Reeves has been spending unusual amounts of time here.

Known clues:
- Navigator Chen flagged an anomaly: one cargo drone departed 6 hours before the incident — off-schedule and unlogged.
- A hidden panel in the hold contains a personal comms device — encrypted — not registered to any crew member.

Secrets (Game Master only):
- The comms device belongs to Kai. It was used to transmit Veilite sample data to Axiom Industries. The off-schedule drone carried a physical sample container.

# Location: Escape Pod Bay (Section E)
type: emergency
status: two pods operational, one pod — bay door manually locked
access_level: emergency_use

Summary: Three escape pods. Pod 1 and 2 are operational and stocked. Pod 3's bay door has been manually locked from the outside — no fault logged, no maintenance order.

Known clues:
- The lock on Pod 3 was applied at 01:45 — 29 minutes before Airlock 7 opened.
- Pod 3 is the pod closest to Section C. Whoever opened Airlock 7 wanted to make sure Voss couldn't escape.

Secrets (Game Master only):
- Kai locked Pod 3 before going to Section C. It was deliberate. He wanted no escape route.
