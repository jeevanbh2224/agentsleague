## Summary
ISS Tartarus is a deep-space relay and mining-support station owned and operated by HeliosCore Corporation, located at Lagrange Point 4 of the Kepler-452 system — approximately 1,400 light-years from Earth. Station construction was completed in 2157. Continuous crewed operation has run for seven years.

## Physical Structure
The station is a modular ring design with six primary sections:
- **Section A** — Command Deck, Navigation, Communications
- **Section B** — Engineering, Reactor Core, Life Support
- **Section C** — Science Labs, Greenhouse, Medical Bay *(currently sealed — vacuum breach)*
- **Section D** — Crew Quarters, Mess Hall, Recreation
- **Section E** — Cargo Hold, Equipment Storage, Docking Bay
- **Section F** — ARIA Server Room, Station Archives, Corporate Data Vault

All sections are connected by a central spine corridor. Emergency bulkheads can isolate any section independently.

## Official Station Purpose
HeliosCore states Tartarus is a deuterium extraction relay — collecting deuterium from the gas giant Kepler-452b for fuel synthesis and shipping back via cargo drone.

## Actual Station Purpose (CLASSIFIED — Game Master knowledge only)
HeliosCore is extracting **Veilite** — a crystalline mineral found in the upper mantle of Kepler-452b — which appears to interfere with conventional sensor arrays and has potential military and surveillance applications. Dr. Voss discovered this during routine analysis of mineral samples and confronted Kai Reeves, believing he could help her expose it. Kai instead reported the conversation to an Axiom Industries contact and agreed to silence her.

## The Incident
**Station Day 2888 — 02:14 station time.**
Airlock 7 in Section C was opened from the inside. Section C vented to vacuum. Dr. Mira Voss was the sole occupant. Her body was recovered from Section C six hours later. ARIA reports corrupted telemetry logs for the 02:00–02:30 window.

## Atmosphere and Tone
Isolated. Cold. Claustrophobic. The nearest rescue vessel is 14 days away. Oxygen reserves are at 82% following the Section C loss. Every decision carries weight. Trust is scarce. The corporation is watching — HeliosCore monitors all encrypted transmissions from the station.

## Laws and Protocols
- Corporate Law: HeliosCore Security Protocol 7 — any crew member accused of sabotage may be detained without formal charges pending corporate investigation.
- Station Law: Commander has full authority over crew safety decisions during emergencies.
- Emergency Protocol: If oxygen drops below 40%, all non-essential crew enter hibernation pods.
- ARIA Directive 1: Protect station assets and corporate data above crew directives unless human life is at immediate risk.
