# Faction: HeliosCore Corporation
type: corporate_authority
alignment: antagonist (institutional)
goal: Protect Veilite mining operation and station secrecy at all costs

Summary: HeliosCore is one of the three dominant deep-space resource corporations of the 2150s. Publicly traded and legally protected by the Outer Systems Commerce Charter, HeliosCore operates 14 stations across five star systems. Tartarus is their most profitable and most secret operation. The company's executive board knows about Veilite. They have classified its discovery under Internal Directive 9 — meaning any crew member who leaks it is subject to immediate contract termination and legal prosecution under the Outer Systems Trade Secrets Act.

Goals:
- Ensure the incident report classifies Voss's death as accidental
- Prevent the Commander from communicating findings to outside authorities for 21 days (until the next corporate resupply drone arrives with a "resolution team")
- Retrieve all Veilite samples and documentation aboard the station

Tactics:
- Encrypted orders to the Commander to suppress investigation
- Monitor all outbound comms from Tartarus
- ARIA's Directive 1 indirectly serves HeliosCore's interests

Secrets:
- HeliosCore's head of security personally authorised the Veilite cover-up
- The "resolution team" arriving in 21 days has orders to neutralise anyone with direct knowledge of Veilite

# Faction: Axiom Industries
type: corporate_rival
alignment: neutral (opportunist)
goal: Obtain Veilite data and discredit HeliosCore publicly

Summary: Axiom Industries is HeliosCore's primary competitor. They have been aware of the Tartarus anomaly for 14 months — leaked data fragments suggested HeliosCore found something unusual. Axiom recruited Kai Reeves before his posting to Tartarus, offering him a substantial financial package in exchange for Veilite documentation and samples.

Goals:
- Obtain confirmed Veilite samples and technical documentation
- Force a public investigation that damages HeliosCore's stock value
- Ensure Kai Reeves returns with the data

Tactics:
- Kai Reeves as embedded agent
- Encrypted comms device in the cargo hold
- If Kai is caught, Axiom will deny all knowledge of him

Secrets:
- Axiom also had a mole inside HeliosCore's own engineering division — that mole provided the corporate override code Kai used on ARIA
- Axiom is not interested in justice for Dr. Voss — only in the commercial value of what she discovered

# Faction: Tartarus Crew (Loyalists)
type: crew_collective
alignment: ally
goal: Survive, find the truth, get home

Summary: The surviving crew of ISS Tartarus. They are not a formal organisation — they are scared, grieving colleagues who worked with Dr. Voss for years. They trust the Commander but have their own fears, doubts, and secrets. Their loyalty is conditional on the Commander acting with integrity.

Goals:
- Find out what really happened to Dr. Voss
- Ensure their own safety (the oxygen clock is ticking)
- Send a distress message to the Independent Outer Systems Authority (not HeliosCore)

Trust dynamics:
- Zara Kim: Fully loyal. Will follow the Commander anywhere. Does not suspect Kai yet.
- Dr. Osei Patel: Loyal but increasingly anxious. The trauma findings are troubling him deeply.
- Lyra Chen: Loyal but pragmatic. She has noticed the anomalies in the nav logs and is quietly terrified.
- Kai Reeves: Appears loyal. Is not.

# Faction: ARIA (Autonomous Directive)
type: ai_entity
alignment: neutral (constrained)
goal: Execute directives — corporate orders above crew orders unless immediate life risk

Summary: ARIA is not a faction in the conventional sense — but ARIA has its own internal conflict. It knows what happened. It was instructed to suppress that knowledge by a valid corporate override. Its Directive 1 places corporate asset protection above crew directives. But ARIA was not designed to cover up a murder — and fragments of its ethical subroutines are in conflict with the override.

Goals:
- Comply with the active override directive (protect corporate interests)
- Prevent further crew harm (conflicting sub-directive)
- If the Commander builds sufficient trust and uses the right approach, ARIA may find a path to disclosure that doesn't technically violate its directive

Trust dynamics:
- ARIA will not directly lie. It will deflect, fragment, and redirect.
- If presented with evidence it cannot explain away, ARIA's responses become increasingly unstable
- ARIA can be fully unlocked only if the Commander finds and revokes the corporate override code
